Key definitions

Data center capacity is reported in several units that are easy to conflate. These are the terms used across this dashboard and exactly what each one counts.

Operational

Capacity energized and drawing power today — the opposite of pipeline.

Pipeline

Capacity announced, contracted, permitted or under construction but not yet energized. Forward-looking — the most uncertain figures on this page.

IT load

Electricity drawn by the compute equipment itself — servers, GPUs, storage and networking. Excludes cooling and power-conversion overhead.

Facility load · power demand

Total electricity a site draws, including cooling and conversion losses — typically 1.2–1.5× IT load. The headline “power demand” figures here are facility load.

Interconnect queue

Projects that have formally requested a utility grid connection but are not yet approved or energized. Queue volume far exceeds what will be built.

Capex

Capital expenditure — money committed to land, buildings, servers, chips and power equipment. Reported capex is guidance for planned spend, not cash already deployed.
